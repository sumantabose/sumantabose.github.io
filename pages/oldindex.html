I am a PhD Student in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering at Nanyang Technological University (NTU) Singapore. My research is supervised by Assoc. Prof. Fan Weijun and Prof. Dao Hua Zhang, at NTU Singapore. I am a team member of the Centre for OptoElectronics and Biophotonics (OPTIMUS), at NTU Singapore.

I also work in collaboration with the group of Asst. Prof. Cuong Dang and Prof. Hilmi Volkan Demir from Luminous! Center of Excellence for Semiconductor Lighting and Displays and Institute of Materials Science and Nanotechnology (UNAM), Bilkent University, Turkey.

My primary research area for PhD is mathematical modeling and simulation of semiconductor quantum nanostructures such as quantum dots/wires/wells and nanoplatelets. I also work on algorithms in machine learning, robotics, embedded systems and image processing. Previously I have worked on free space optics, metamaterials, antennas, electromagnetics and digital communication system. Across the area I've worked on I've extensively used probability, statistics, linear algebra, numerical methods, etc. implementing them on various platforms/languages such as C, C++, Python, Fortran, Matlab to name some. My publications are enlisted here.

Prior joining NTU Singapore, I graduated with the degree of Bachelor of Technology (B. Tech) from the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering in National Institute of Technology Trichy, India with First Class honours in 2013.

Apart from research, I am interested in travelling as a backpacker, reading, non-academic writing and film-making. Currently I am writing a fiction novel and a travellogue. As a student leader, I've also served as the Lead Curator of TEDxNTU, which is the TED (Technology, Entertainment, Design) chapter of NTU Singapore. Check out our past event videos.
